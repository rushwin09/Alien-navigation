Understanding Humans

Humans are complex social beings with fascinating customs. Here's what you need to know to blend in — or at least not cause an intergalactic incident.

🤝

Greetings & Contact

Humans greet each other by shaking hands, hugging, or waving. Physical contact norms vary wildly by region.

Social Structures

Humans organize into families, communities, and nations. They have complex hierarchies and a concept called "personal space."

Survival Protocol

Essential Survival Guide

Critical information to keep you alive and functional on this planet. Read carefully — Earth is more dangerous than it looks.

🌬️

Atmosphere & Breathing

▼

Earth's atmosphere is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases. Oxygen is required by most life forms here.

  • Atmospheric pressure: 101.325 kPa at sea level
  • Breathable up to ~8,000 meters altitude
  • UV radiation is present — protective measures advised
  • Air composition varies in urban areas (pollution)
⚠ If your species breathes methane, you will need a personal atmosphere unit at all times.
⬇️

Gravity & Movement

▼

Earth's gravity is 9.8 m/s². This may feel heavy or light depending on your home planet.

  • Falling from heights is dangerous — humans cannot fly
  • Bodies of water have different buoyancy properties
  • Move carefully until calibrated — humans walk bipedally
  • Expect fatigue if your home gravity is lower than 9.8 m/s²
🥤

Food & Water

▼

Humans require H₂O (water) and organic nutrients to survive. They acquire these through "meals."

  • Water: Clear liquid, found almost everywhere. Drink the purified kind.
  • Food: Comes in countless forms. "Restaurants" are food distribution centers.
  • Avoid: Anything labeled "poison," cleaning products, or raw meat
  • Popular safe options: bread, rice, fruits, cooked vegetables
⚠ Some humans have "allergies" — adverse reactions to certain foods. Scan before consuming.
🏠

Shelter & Rest

▼

Humans build enclosed structures for protection from weather and temperature extremes.

  • "Hotels" provide temporary shelter in exchange for currency
  • Temperature inside is regulated between 18-24°C typically
  • Humans sleep 7-9 hours on soft platforms called "beds"
  • Shelter is essential — exposure to elements can be fatal
💳

Currency & Trade

▼

Humans use a complex exchange system based on abstract "money." They don't barter — they use numbers.

  • Different regions use different currencies (USD, EUR, JPY, etc.)
  • Digital payments are increasingly common (cards, phones)
  • Resources are NOT free — everything has a "price"
  • "Free" things that exist: air, most water bodies, sunlight, public parks
🚀

Transportation

▼

Humans use primitive but functional vehicles to move across their planet's surface.

  • "Cars" — 4-wheeled ground vehicles. Dangerous at speed.
  • "Trains" — Linked carriages on metal tracks. Efficient.
  • "Airplanes" — Yes, they actually fly. Slow but reliable.
  • "Buses" — Large communal ground transport.
  • They follow "traffic rules" — colored light signals (red=stop, green=go).
⚠ Do NOT use your personal spacecraft in populated areas. It draws unwanted attention.
